User Research

Back 2 School is a new product so I needed to understand the potential users more so that I know more about their frustrations and expectations then curate a product that suits them best. I decide to conduct user surveys using google forms and send them out to parents with children still going to school

These are the questions asked:

  1. Do you want your kids to go to a public or private school?
  2. Did the school meet your expectations?
  3. What are some common thinking styles parents demonstrate when making a school choice?
  4. How much time do parents typically spend researching different schools? How many schools do they consider before making a choice?
  5. What would parents change about the school choice process?

Exploring Solutions

After conducting the survey we found out that most parents liked the fact that they could read reviews of other parents, it gave the digital platform a better user experience.

We also realised that some parents didnt necesarily want to book atour for every scholl they were interested in, sometimes they just wanted to call or message the school administration to find out more or clarify something they wer unsure of. We added a voice call and direct message feature at the top of the pages with the school content.

A parents always has certain qualities that they look for in a school before they enroll their child there. They needed a filtering option so that they get the search results that they want to go through only which prevents them from going through other results and wasting their time. We added the filter and sort feature near the search button.

Parents also needed a reminder when they booked a tour online using the digital platform. Most of them forgot to manually set reminders and wanted the tour to be added to their calendars automatically when they finish booking a school tour.

There is video section that allows the parents to interact with video content about the schools they are thinking of choosing. With the videos they are able to see what really happens on the ground and if what they say they have is actually there, They can also have virtual tours using the videos.
