IN THE BAG

There is an easier way to teach your kids about money without shouting at them.

Role and Collaboration

I was the only user researcher in this project. I had to figure out the pain points, the delights and the characteristics of our potential users which were parents and kids who were 9-13 years old. I also needed to go through existing products that were offering financial literacy to kids and find out wht can be improved.

What is 'In the Bag'?

Building Early Money Habits in Kids. In the Bag is a digital platform that provides age based, interactive money lessons for children 10 -12 years old (5th and 6th grade). It is a 5 week interactive live course taught online via Zoom where children learn about personal finance.

Background

According to Forbes, 50% of children surveyed wish their parents taught them more about money. Children are not equipped with financial literacy and yet they are magically expected to be good at managing money when they are adults. They end up wasting money getting into debt which eventually affects their mental health and other aspects of their life. In the bag is a digital platform that will provide children with financial management resources that make break down the concepts to be easy to understand and fun to learn

Research Methodology

The were three groups of participants for the interviews and surveys: parents, teachers and the 9-12 years children.

Learn more about the attention span of kids aged 9 to 12 years old.

The importance of breaks in between classes in children aged 9 to 12.

Insights

Savings and budgeting are the most important topics parents want their children to learn.

The classes should be interactive and include games and videos as their main medium of teaching the children because most children learn using visuals and touch.

Parents lack support on financial management platforms for their children. All they can do is view their kids progress.

Children at that age require to be encouraged and reassured frequently because they can get impatient.

Visuals

Most parents want their 9-13 year old children to learn the basics of saving and budgeting. They also want their children to be able to differenciate between a want and a need so they can prioritize spending on needs before wants.

Majority of the kids have their own devices and can access the platform with their own credentials, but we must still consider those who will be using their parent’s devices and will log in using their parent’s credentials. We must have profiles that will enable all kids to log in using their parent’s credentails and have their own data on their personalised profile.

The majority are auditory and kinesthetic learners which means we must iclude some assignments where the parents help their children act out real life scenarios like borrowing a loan so that they can touch the money which favours kinesthetic learners and listen to what is being said.

Recommendations

The classes should be interactive and include games and videos as their main medium of teaching the children because most children learn using visuals and touch.

Parents should also be supported by creating educational resources for them to learn about financial management too. They can have webinars and listen to investment talk about different investment plans they can venture into.

The platform can enable the child to set a goal for the day like going through two videos about savings and once the goal is achieved the are rewarded with starts or bonus virtual money.

The platform can also have daily streaks to motivate the children to visit the platform at least least once everyday.
