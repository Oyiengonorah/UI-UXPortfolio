MY WORK

Qualitative and Quantitative Research

Conducted remote user interviews via google meet and surveys using google forms. I then used the data I collected from the interviews and surveys to inform my design decisions and feature prioritization.

Composition and Information Design

Using a bigger font for headings to show urgency or importance and draw the attention of the viewer or ensuring the information is arranged in a symmetrical manner to enhance composition of a page. Increasing the experience of the user which helps in retaining them.

Analytics and Presenting Insights

Creating affinity diagrams, user personnas and graphs using Figma and Microsoft Excel to visualize and analyze the data I have collected from the research and make meaning out of it. It helps in coming up ith the insights that will ifnorm design desicions.

Typography and Colour

Doing secondary research about the meaning of different colours and typography and choose those that align with the product's objectives. Increasing the trust and acceptance of the product by the consumers.

Customer Journey Maps, user personas and JBTD maps

Creating different mental models that I use to present the research insights to different stakeholders. Increasing the trust and understanding of the stakeholders of how the research process is creating a solution for their product.

Wireframing and Prototyping

Creating interactive low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes in Figma using Grids and Auto-layout from hand drawn sketches that are used in Usability testing and also to show the client what you have come up with.

Information Architecture

Using the F patterns and writing content for skimming by using lists and highlighting the important areas. The users are able to continue looking at other pages of the product and take the information that they need.

Usability Testing

Conducting user testing by asking the users to complete certain tasks with the product to check the usability and understanding of the product before launching it and changing the areas where the users encountered problems.

HTML, CSS and Javascript
